About

I am a PhD-trained materials and process R&D scientist with 6+ years of experience developing and scaling functional nanomaterials for battery materials, hydrogen production, and catalysis. My work links atomic-level materials design to device-level performance — and to manufacturable processes.

I earned my Ph.D. in Chemical and Biological Engineering at Seoul National University under Prof. Taeghwan Hyeon, followed by a postdoctoral fellowship at the IBS Center for Nanoparticle Research. At Argonne National Laboratory, I now operate continuous supercritical-water hydrothermal reactors and pilot-scale spray dryers to produce Li-ion (LFP, LMFP, LMR) and Na-ion battery materials at up to 300 g/h.

My research has been published in Nature Materials, JACS, Chem, Joule, Advanced Materials, and Energy & Environmental Science, and recognized with the Samsung Humantech Paper Award.
